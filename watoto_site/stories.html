Our Story

Watoto are Tomorrow was founded in 2019 with a vision to restore hope to the displaced and forgotten refugee children who faced the harsh reality of shattered dreams. It was a team of passionate founders who came together after realizing the immense need for a change in the lives of refugee children. Many of these children had been denied access to formal education due to the language barriers they faced, and as a result, their futures seemed uncertain and bleak.

One pivotal moment that sparked the initiative was when one of the founders witnessed their own sibling facing the devastating consequences of early pregnancy. This served as a profound turning point, one that ignited a deep desire to make a difference in society. It was then that they took the brave step to act, determined to create a platform where young people—especially the most vulnerable—could be empowered to envision a future full of promise.

What began as a simple English training center using videos and animations to teach children aged 3 to 17 grew into a beacon of hope for thousands. These children, who were previously unable to access formal education, began to learn and grow through innovative methods that bridged the gap between them and the world around them. The lessons were designed not only to teach language but to open doors to a better future—one where these children would have the tools they needed to achieve their dreams.

In 2020, the work of "Watoto are Tomorrow" received major recognition when it was awarded the prestigious Award of Recognition by Response Innovation Lab and Save the Children, acknowledging the organization's groundbreaking work as an innovative solution in Uganda. This recognition not only validated the efforts of the founders but also provided the momentum needed to continue pushing boundaries.

By 2022, the organization had reached a milestone: the establishment of the Watoto Hub Learning Model. With two official learning hubs and five periodical ones, Watoto are Tomorrow has become a beacon of educational empowerment, offering refuge and growth for many children who were once forgotten. The hubs are not just places of learning—they are vibrant centers where dreams are reignited, and futures are transformed.

To date, over 300 refugees have been empowered, their lives forever changed by the education, support, and guidance they received. And this is only the beginning. Watoto are Tomorrow has an ambitious vision for the future, with even more plans in the pipeline to expand their reach and impact, ensuring that no child is left behind and that the dream of a brighter tomorrow is made real for all.

Watoto are Tomorrow isn't just a learning center; it's a movement that believes in the infinite potential of every child, regardless of their background. The journey continues, and with each step, more children are given the chance to rise above their circumstances and become the change-makers of tomorrow.
